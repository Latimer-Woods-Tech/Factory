Process

Straight line from first conversation to handoff.

No black-box dev cycle. No vague retainer. No mystery around what “done” means.

01

Fit check

You tell me what you are building and what is blocked. I tell you whether it is a fit, what I would scope, and where I would not take the work.

02

Scoped outcome

You get a short scope document with deliverables, timeline, fixed price, and explicit out-of-scope boundaries.

03

Build in the open

The work happens in a repo you own, with visible pull requests, real deployments, and no dependency theater.

04

Production verification

Done means the system is live and healthy. If the custom domain cannot answer cleanly, the work is not finished.

05

Operate without me

You leave with the runbook, secret rotation path, monitoring context, and enough clarity to keep moving after handoff.

Fit

Best for deliberate builders.

Good fit

  • Founders who want a real backend instead of a future rewrite
  • Teams shipping AI features without infra support around them
  • Media businesses that need repeatable production instead of manual pipelines

Not a fit

  • Projects that want a fast prototype with no production intent
  • Teams that need a full-time embedded employee
  • Work that depends on vague scope and endless revisions
  • Architectures that assume the real cleanup can happen later
