How it works

Straightforward from first contact to handoff.

No retainer, no ongoing dependency unless you want one. Each engagement has a defined scope, a defined outcome, and ends when that outcome is deployed and working.

01

Initial conversation

You describe what you're building and what's blocked. I tell you whether it's a fit, what I'd scope it to, and roughly what it would cost. No obligation, usually 30 minutes.

02

Scoping document

I write a short document: deliverables list, what's in scope, what's out, timeline, and fixed price. You review it and decide. No surprises later.

03

Build in the open

Work happens in a GitHub repo you own from day one. Every PR is visible, every deploy is verified. No black-box development, no "trust me it'll be done soon."

04

Verified delivery

Done means curl https://your-domain.com/health returns 200 and you've walked through the running system with me. Not "CI is green" — actually working in production.

05

Handoff with runbook

You get a runbook: how to deploy, how to rotate secrets, what to watch in Sentry, how to scale. The goal is that you can operate it without me after I leave.

Fit

This works well for some projects. Not all of them.

Good fit

  • Seed or pre-seed founders who need a production backend fast and cheap
  • Indie builders replacing expensive AWS / Vercel setups
  • Product teams adding AI features without bandwidth to do it correctly
  • Content businesses wanting automated video without manual production
  • Anyone who values clean, auditable, well-documented infrastructure over speed shortcuts

Not a fit

  • Projects requiring Node.js-specific runtimes or Docker-based deploys
  • Large teams that need an embedded full-time employee
  • Anyone looking for a quick prototype with no production intent
  • Projects where "we'll figure out the architecture later" is the plan
  • Anything with unclear scope and no fixed deliverable in mind
